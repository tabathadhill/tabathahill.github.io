Case Study 01

Translating Expertise
into Actionable Learning

Sales Enablement Instructional Design Cross-functional Multi-modal Content SME Partnership

As the organization scaled, Sales and Service Delivery teams needed consistent, role-specific learning resources. I owned the end-to-end translation from SME expertise to usable learning assets — bridging both teams to reduce rework and accelerate ramp-up.

Context & Business Need

As the organization scaled, Sales and Service Delivery teams needed consistent learning resources for an expansion sales learning initiative — including a playbook, microlearnings, and a discovery question bank.

The business needed enablement that could serve multiple audiences with different needs from the same core knowledge, while reducing repeat questions and interruptions to subject matter experts.

Capture SME expertise without requiring SMEs to act as trainers
Serve Sales, Service Delivery, managers, and new hires from one shared knowledge base
Reduce repeat questions and SME interruptions
Improve alignment between what Sales promised and what Service delivered
Support faster ramp-up and consistent execution across regions and roles

My Role

I served as Enablement and Learning Content Partner, acting as the bridge between SMEs, Sales, and Service Delivery. I owned the full translation from expertise to usable learning assets, including needs discovery, content design, development, alignment reviews, and iteration post-launch.

Approach

01

SME Partnership and Knowledge Extraction

Facilitated focused working sessions to capture workflows, common issues, best practices, decision points, and what good looks like. Applied performance-based questioning to uncover where people struggle and structured SME input so they contributed efficiently without needing to build content themselves.

02

Multi-modality Content Design for Workflow Fit

Developed assets based on audience and use case, including short eLearning modules for foundational knowledge, job aids and quick reference guides for just-in-time support, scenario-based examples for real sales and service situations, process walkthroughs, and manager support materials.

03

Cross-functional Alignment: Sales and Service Delivery

Validated accuracy with both groups and resolved inconsistencies. Ensured Sales messaging reflected service delivery realities and Service teams understood upstream commitments, building shared expectations to reduce confusion and rework.

04

Feedback and Iteration

Collected learner and manager feedback after launch, identified gaps, updated unclear areas, and iterated quickly. Treated enablement as living resources rather than one-time deliverables.

Outcomes

Consistent, role-aligned learning resources used across Sales and Service Delivery
Faster ramp-up for new and transitioning team members
Reduced SME dependency for repeat questions
Increased confidence applying knowledge on the job
Stronger alignment between what was sold and what was delivered
Practical, usable tools that supported real execution
